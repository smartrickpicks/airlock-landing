Open Positions
Challengers
Engineering Challenger ▾
"Most companies want people who agree faster. We want people who disagree better. A Challenger at Airlock isn't contrarian for sport — they're the person who makes every idea survive contact with reality. You're not difficult. You're expensive to ignore."
What You'll Do
  • Stress-test architecture decisions before they ship
  • Review code, benchmarks, and system designs with a "what breaks this?" mindset
  • Build the things that need building — and prevent the things that shouldn't
  • Own technical due diligence on new features and integrations
  • Push back on the founder's engineering assumptions with evidence
You Might Be Right For This If
  • You've killed a project that everyone else was excited about — and you were right
  • You read technical blog posts and your first thought is what they left out
  • You care more about whether something should exist than whether you can build it
  • You've built systems and broken systems, and learned more from breaking them
You're Probably Not Right For This If
  • You need a detailed spec before you start thinking
  • You wait for someone to assign you work
  • You think "devil's advocate" is a personality
  • You argue to win, not to find the truth
Strategy Challenger ▾
"Most companies want people who agree faster. We want people who disagree better. A Challenger at Airlock isn't contrarian for sport — they're the person who makes every idea survive contact with reality. You're not difficult. You're expensive to ignore."
What You'll Do
  • Pressure-test business strategy, go-to-market plan, and fundraising approach
  • Research competitive landscape and bring back intel that changes decisions
  • Model scenarios — what if X fails, what's Plan B, what are we not seeing
  • Own the hard questions: unit economics, customer acquisition, pricing, market timing
  • Be the person who says "that won't work because..." with a better alternative ready
You Might Be Right For This If
  • You've talked someone out of a bad business decision and they thanked you later
  • You read pitch decks for fun and always find the gap
  • You've worked where being wrong was expensive — consulting, finance, military, operations
  • You think strategy without stress-testing is just storytelling
You're Probably Not Right For This If
  • You need consensus before you share an opinion
  • You think the founder's job is to have all the answers
  • You're more comfortable executing a plan than questioning one
  • Ambiguity paralyzes you
